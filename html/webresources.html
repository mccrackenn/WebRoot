Codecademy

Codecademy
Codecademy- Codecademy has been and continues to be a valuable learning tool in my progression towards becoming a full-stack developer. I paid for a one year pro membership back in December and I have not regretted my choice. Codecademy’s learning model is multi-faceted. They give you written lessons, followed by a simulator where you practice what you just learned. To further cement the lesson, one completes projects after the lessons. These projects offer hints if you get stuck, along with an option to watch a recorded video of a professional developer completing the project. Rating:5 out of 5.

Tutorialspoint

Tutorialspoint
Tutorialspoint is a site I often look to when I need more information on a topic. Their webpage structure is excellent. A menu on the left side of the page concisely structures the learning module, making it simple to narrow in on what you want. For instance, just this morning I was seeking help on Bootstrap. I was easily able to find the grid system information I was looking for because grid system was one of choices on the menu. Much like W3 schools, Tutorialspoint provides detailed information on a subject and demonstrates it with snippets of code. A live demo option with various device selectors gives you the ability to see the output on different sized screens. Rating:5 out of 5.

Tim Corey YouTube Channel

Tim Corey
I discovered Tim Corey’s YouTube channel while taking Prog123, Server Side Web Dev at Bellevue College. His videos have been immensely helpful, specifically those on MVC and read/writing to databases. I enjoy the way he breaks down and explains complex ideas. If he has done a video previously on one of these complex ideas, he will provide a link and instruct his subscribers to check it out. He builds projects from ground up to completion. One can sit back and watch the whole tutorial or treat is as a ride-along. Building the project along with him. Rating:4.5 out of 5.

GeeksforGeeks

GeeksforGeeks
4. Geeks for Geeks is another of my go-to sources that is rich and diverse in subject matter. While they do have a tutorials section for web technologies that includes; HTML, CSS, jQuery and JavaScript (listed in both Web Technologies & Languages). I have found myself on the site more for their robust programming language examples e.g. writing functions, OOP, than for support with HTML, CSS problems. For beginners to intermediate programmers, Geeks does a fantastic job correlating clear examples from the standards, which are not overly complicated. Rating:4 out of 5.

Stack Overflow

Stack Overflow
Stack Overflow is a resource that often either helps me or leads me further down the rabbit hole in my search. This is because many of the questions/solutions on Stack are above my level. Hopefully as I advance in my education/career, I can grasp more from the site. Whenever I do a Google search for help with an issue, Stack Overflow is usually the top result or one of the top five results. Due to the complexity of many examples, I try to use the site as support if I cannot find an answer on my other web resource sites. Rating:3.5 out of 5.
